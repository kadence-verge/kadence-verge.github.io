Hip-Hop is a genre of popular music that originated in the United States in the late 1970s and early 1980s. It is characterized by rapping, a rhythmic rhyming chanted vocalization, and manipulation of songs with turntables and, later, computer applications made to edit music and synthesize rhythms.

The second wave of Hip-Hop music hit in the mid 1980s and was exemplified by the music of LL Cool J. Hip-Hop's golden age lasted from the beginning of the second wave to the early 1990s. After the golden age, Hip-Hop diversified. Gangsta' rap focussed on the violent life of inner city youth particularly in African American and Latin-American communities. After the golden age, Hip-Hop suffered a loss in popularity. Notable exceptions are Eminem, Dr. Dre. Since, Hip-Hop has merged with pop in the form of crossover music, like that of Kanye West.

Hip-Hop music is often associated with the Hip-Hop movement of the late 1970s. It is one of the four characteristics of the period, the others being break dancing, graffiti writing, and Disc Jockeying.

"Rapper's Delight," by the Sugarhill Gang, is generally accepted as the first Hip-Hop song.
