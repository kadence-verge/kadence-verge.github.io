Heavy Metal is a genre of music spawned from rock n roll beginning in the 1970’s. Pioneers like Led Zepplin and Black Sabbath paved the way for further subgenres.

With roots in blues and rock, the bands that created heavy metal developed a thick, massive sound, characterized by highly amplified distortion, extended guitar solos, emphatic beats, and overall loudness. Heavy metal lyrics and performance styles are often associated with masculinity, aggression, and machismo.

The electric guitar has always been key in heavy metal, usually very loud and distorted. Palm muting is often employed to keep the sound tighter and more precise. Loud and dense bass and drums are also usually present. Overall, loudness is key.

A good example of heavy metal is the song “Master of Puppets” by Metallica. Metallica is a classic thrash metal band, a subgenre of heavy metal emphasized by speed and aggression.
